Who I am.

I'm junior at UC Berkeley double majoring in Computer Science and Integrative Biology. I am super interested in the intersection of computer science and healthcare/medicine.

I never really thought about majoring in Computer Science before Cal until I took my first programming class and absolutely fell in love and realized how powerful of a tool it is and how applicable it is to biology, healthcare, and research.

What I've done.

I'm super passionate about discovery and I have both a research background in wet and dry lab.

In my wet lab experience, I led a team of PhD and Masters students in Martine Culty's Reproductive Toxicology lab at the USC School of Pharmacy where I looked at the combination of the effect of endocrine disruptors on spermatogonia and testicular macrophage cells co-authored a presentation for national andrology conference and gained media recognition for my work.

I've also worked in a startup where we tested different chemical compounds to see which impaired the mitochondria for spermatogonia in hopes of creating unisex contraception under Nadja Mannowetz CSO of formerly YourChoice Therapeutics now known as Anna Therapeutics.

In my dry lab experience, I worked with a team to develop a classifier to identify intrinsically disordered and ordered regions in proteins using a neural network in Keras written in Python under PhD candidate Marc Singleton.
